# Content License and Contribution Terms

## Scope

This document applies to crowdsourced website content, including entries, definitions, examples, annotations, tags, moderation notes intended for publication, and media uploaded by contributors.

- User-contributed public content is licensed under `CC BY-SA 4.0`.
- By submitting content, contributors confirm they have the right to submit it and to license it under `CC BY-SA 4.0`.
- Reusers must provide attribution and share derivative works under the same license terms.

## Contributor Warranties

By submitting content, the contributor represents that:

- the submission does not knowingly violate third-party rights;
- the submission is lawful in the contributor's jurisdiction;
- personally identifying or sensitive information is not shared without consent.

## Moderation and Curation Rights

Project maintainers may review, edit, annotate, verify, unpublish, or remove submitted content for legal, safety, quality, or policy reasons. Moderation decisions are part of operating a safe public archive.

## Attribution Model

- The platform should preserve creator attribution metadata where available.
- When attribution cannot be displayed safely, maintainers may pseudonymize or redact visible attribution while preserving internal audit metadata.

## Takedown and Corrections

- Contributors may request correction, anonymization, or removal of their submissions where feasible and lawful.
- Maintainers may process takedowns to address legal risk, safety risk, or credible rights claims.

This document should be updated as moderation APIs, reporting workflows, and verification notifications are implemented.
